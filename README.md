## 💡 Business Insights

> Clothing (44.73%) and Accessories (31.83%) together contribute **76.56% of total revenue** making them the two most critical categories for business survival and growth.

> Blouse ($10,410), Shirt ($10,332), Dress ($10,320), Pants ($10,090) and Jewelry ($10,010) generate almost equal revenue showing a **healthy product mix with no single product dependency**.

> Male customers (2,652) outnumber female (1,248) by 2x but **average spend is almost equal** ($59.54 vs $60.25) suggesting female customers are equally valuable per transaction despite smaller numbers.

> Customers with discounts spend **$59.28 on average vs $60.13 without discounts** — a $0.85 reduction per order. Discounts are NOT increasing spending and may be eroding profit margins.

> Purchase frequency is spread evenly across all frequencies with weekly buyers at just 13.82%. A **loyalty rewards program** could shift customers from quarterly to monthly buying frequency.

> All 6 payment methods are used almost equally (15.69% to 17.36%) showing **no single payment method dominates**. Offering equal rewards across all payment methods is recommended over single-payment promotions.

> Size M accounts for **45% of all purchases** while XL is only 11% — suggesting either stock shortages or limited variety in XL sizes representing an **untapped market opportunity**.
